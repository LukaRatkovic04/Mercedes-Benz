2023: Focus on Electric Vehicles: Mercedes-Benz remained committed to its electric vehicle strategy, with BEV sales almost doubling in the first half of 2023. The company presented several new electric models, including the Mercedes-Maybach EQS SUV and the Mercedes-EQS SUV.

2005: Mercedes-Benz launches the Mercedes-Benz S 400 Hybrid, the first production car with a hybrid drive system. This groundbreaking vehicle combined a gasoline engine with an electric motor, significantly improving fuel economy and reducing emissions, paving the way for a new era of environmentally conscious luxury automobiles.

1976: Mercedes-Benz pioneers a new era of automotive safety with the introduction of the airbag in the Mercedes-Benz W123. This life-saving technology, now standard in modern vehicles, demonstrates the brand's commitment to passenger protection and its dedication to advancing safety measures.

1931: Mercedes-Benz introduces the revolutionary Mercedes-Benz 300, a grand touring car that sets new standards for luxury and performance. This model showcases the brand's commitment to engineering excellence and innovation.

1886: In Mannheim, Germany, Carl Benz unveils the Patent Motorwagen, widely recognized as the world's first self-propelled automobile. This groundbreaking invention marks the beginning of Mercedes-Benz's legacy as a pioneer in automotive innovation.
